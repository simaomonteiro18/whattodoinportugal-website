The Most Instagrammable Places in Portugal

March 2026 · 7 min read

Portugal is one of those countries where every corner looks like it was designed for a photograph. Between the golden light that bathes Lisbon in the late afternoon, the dramatic cliffs of the Algarve, and the fairytale palaces of Sintra, your camera roll is going to fill up fast. Here are the most photogenic spots you absolutely cannot miss.

Palácio da Pena, Sintra

Rising above the misty forests of the Serra de Sintra, the Palácio da Pena looks like something straight out of a Disney film. Its bold red, yellow, and purple facades contrast beautifully against the lush green surroundings, making every angle a postcard-worthy shot. The terraces offer sweeping views all the way to the Atlantic Ocean on clear days. Arrive early in the morning for the best light and fewer crowds in your photos.

Poço Iniciático, Quinta da Regaleira

This spiral underground tower, descending nine levels into the earth, is one of the most mysterious and photogenic spots in all of Portugal. The Initiation Well at Quinta da Regaleira in Sintra was used in Masonic initiation rites, and looking up or down the spiral staircase creates a mesmerising, almost surreal image. The surrounding gardens are equally stunning, with hidden tunnels, grottos, and fountains.

Tram 28, Lisbon

The iconic yellow tram winding through Lisbon's narrow, hilly streets is perhaps the most photographed scene in the city. The best spots to capture it are along the Alfama neighbourhood, where the tram squeezes through impossibly tight streets lined with colourful tiles and hanging laundry. For the best shot, position yourself on a steep street and wait for the tram to climb towards you.

Estação de São Bento, Porto

This railway station in the heart of Porto is covered floor-to-ceiling with around 20,000 hand-painted azulejo tiles depicting scenes from Portuguese history. The blue and white tiles create a stunning visual impact that photographs beautifully. It's free to enter, and the morning light streaming through the windows creates a magical atmosphere.

Discover Porto's tile-covered churches and hidden viewpoints on our Porto Full Day Tour.

Benagil Cave, Algarve

This sea cave with a natural skylight in its ceiling is one of the most iconic images of Portugal. The circular opening lets sunlight pour onto the small beach below, creating an almost cathedral-like effect. You can reach it by boat, kayak, or paddleboard from the nearby Benagil beach. The light is best around midday when the sun shines directly through the opening.

Our Algarve Full Day Tour includes the option to visit Benagil Cave by boat and explore the stunning coastline.

Ponta da Piedade, Lagos

These dramatic golden limestone cliffs, sea stacks, and grottos near Lagos are arguably the most spectacular coastal scenery in Europe. The warm golden rock against the turquoise water creates incredible colour contrasts. Walk along the clifftop path for aerial views, or take a boat trip to see the formations from sea level, passing through natural arches and hidden caves.

Livraria Lello, Porto

Often cited as one of the most beautiful bookshops in the world, Livraria Lello features a stunning neo-Gothic interior with a famous crimson staircase that spirals upward like something from a fantasy novel. It's said to have inspired J.K. Rowling, who lived in Porto while writing the early Harry Potter books. The ornate wooden ceilings, stained glass, and carved bookshelves make every photo look like it belongs in a magazine.

Ribeira do Porto

The colourful houses stacked along the Douro riverfront in Porto, with the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge in the background, create one of Portugal's most recognisable scenes. The best time to photograph it is during golden hour, when the warm light makes the buildings glow. Cross to the Vila Nova de Gaia side for the classic panoramic view, or photograph from one of the many viewpoints along the river.

Azenhas do Mar, Sintra

This tiny whitewashed village perched on a cliff above the Atlantic Ocean is one of the most photographed spots along the Sintra coast. The natural sea pool at the base of the cliffs, with waves crashing against the rocks, creates dramatic images that are hard to believe are real. It's particularly stunning at sunset.

Visit Azenhas do Mar and the dramatic Sintra coast on our Sintra & Coast Full Day Tour.

Cabo da Roca

Standing at the westernmost point of continental Europe, Cabo da Roca offers raw, windswept cliff-top views of the Atlantic that feel like the edge of the world. The lighthouse against the dramatic sky, the rugged cliffs dropping into the ocean below — it's a powerful, atmospheric location that photographs especially well during stormy weather or at sunset.

Óbidos

This perfectly preserved medieval walled town looks like it hasn't changed in centuries. The narrow cobblestone streets lined with whitewashed houses decorated with bougainvillea and blue or yellow trim create an endlessly charming backdrop. Walk along the top of the medieval walls for panoramic views of the terracotta rooftops and the surrounding countryside.
